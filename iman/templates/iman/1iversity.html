Non-monetary Incentives in Crowdwork?

Suri, et al. [1] explain that crowd-sourced jobs do not provide most of the incentives provided in real jobs. E.g., there is no advancement, no reputation, no organization membership, no benefits, no leadership, no identity, no recognition, and no collaboration. These are mosts of the motivators that Karau and Williams [2]' CEM suggest to incorporate in design of online communities to reduce social loafing. Some crowd-sourced platforms have incorporated parts of these incentives in their design or provided the crowd workers with tools to create these types of incentives for themselves. E.g., Amazon Mechanical Turkers share tasks with each other and inform each other about valuable HITs. They also create leadership for specific tasks.

[1] Gray, M. L., Suri, S., Ali, S. S., & Kulkarni, D. (2016, February). The crowd is a collaborative network. In Proceedings of the 19th ACM Conference on Computer-Supported Cooperative Work & Social Computing (pp. 134-147). ACM.

Enterpreneurship at Schools?

From a constructivist learning [1] perspective, schools should encourage ownership and give learners a voice in the learning process. That's part of the reason schools encourage entrepreneurial activities. However, most of course projects are left untouched and there are many barriers to start-up a business among students. They do not know how to access funding, protect or share their intellectual property, breakdown the projects and manage the development process, design, development, marketing, finance, etc. [2] Very few successful ones find and manage a group of their friends with multiple skills and run the start-up, though most of them do not manage to continue their education and business at the same time.

[1] Wilson, B. G. (1996). Constructivist learning environments: Case studies in instructional design. Educational Technology.

[2] Singh Sandhu, M., Fahmi Sidique, S., & Riaz, S. (2011). Entrepreneurship barriers and entrepreneurial inclination among Malaysian postgraduate students. International journal of entrepreneurial behavior & research, 17(4), 428-449.

My Vision for Crowd Startups?

I envision crowd-sourced entrepreneurship at schools. E.g., in an interaction design course students design creative solutions as their projects. They enter their design in the crowd-sourced platform which is shared with other departments at the university. Law students who take intellectual property course select design solutions from the pool of projects and work on them as their course projects. MBA students at strategic management course take projects and divide them into manageable modules. Students at design courses take and work on the design modules; those at programing courses develop the relevant modules; those at marketing courses do the customer discovery; and so on. This way, the school turns into an ecosystem which not only does it teach students, but also it helps them to apply what they learn in real world. At the same time, this platform provides a great tool for investors to easily identify projects to invest in. Moreover, those projects that succeed at school, bring together students from different departments and build friendship among them based on a unified goal. This way they'll be able to easily form the start-up as a business entity after their graduation.
